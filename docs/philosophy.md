# Philosophy of HEI

## The Real Job-to-be-Done

People do not want "emotion detection".

They want this feeling:

> "This AI actually gets me."

Emotion labels are just an intermediate step.  
The real outcome is **feeling understood**.

## Why Current Approaches Fall Short

Most teams solve this with:

- Longer system prompts ("be empathetic, be kind, be warm...")
- Fine-tuning
- Manual trial and error

These methods are:
- Inconsistent across models
- Hard to evaluate
- Difficult to maintain
- Not reusable

## HEI's Core Belief

Emotional intelligence should be **infrastructure**, not prompt engineering.

Just like authentication, payments, and search became reusable layers,  
emotional reasoning should also become a reusable layer that any AI can use.

## Design Principles

1. **Structured reasoning before generation**  
   Don't ask the LLM to "be emotional". Give it a clear emotional plan.

2. **Model-agnostic**  
   The layer must work with GPT, Claude, Grok, Llama, local models, etc.

3. **Honest uncertainty**  
   Never claim "I know exactly how you feel". Always work with confidence scores and tentative language.

4. **Safety by design**  
   Avoid gaslighting, toxic positivity, judgment, and emotional manipulation.

5. **Developer experience first**  
   Simple SDK, clear types, predictable outputs.

## What HEI is Not

- Not a therapist
- Not a mind reader
- Not a lie detector
- Not an emotional surveillance tool

HEI exists to make conversations more human, not to extract or exploit emotions.

## Long-term Vision

Become the standard **Emotional Intelligence Infrastructure** for AI systems —  
the same way Stripe became infrastructure for payments.
